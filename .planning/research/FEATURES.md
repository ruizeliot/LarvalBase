## Differentiators

Features that set apart great facilitators from good ones. These are advanced techniques.

### Advanced Technique Switching

|---------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|--------|
| **Six Thinking Hats** | Parallel thinking eliminates conflict; explores all angles systematically | Color-coded hat icons; structured rotation through perspectives | Medium | [de Bono Group](https://www.debonogroup.com/services/core-programs/six-thinking-hats/), [Toolshero](https://www.toolshero.com/decision-making/six-thinking-hats-de-bono/) |
| **SCAMPER Prompts** | Structured creativity for improving existing ideas | Seven-point prompt wheel; guided modification suggestions | Low | [IxDF SCAMPER](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/learn-how-to-use-the-best-ideation-methods-scamper), [Mindtools](https://www.mindtools.com/ao2rt8j/scamper/) |
| **Reverse Brainstorming** | Breaks through blocks by inverting the problem | "Anti-problem" visual flip; inversion mapping | Medium | [Mindtools Reverse](https://www.mindtools.com/a32qxsh/reverse-brainstorming/), [Miro](https://miro.com/brainstorming/what-is-reverse-brainstorming/) |
| **Starbursting (5W1H)** | Question-first approach ensures comprehensive coverage | Six-point star diagram; question categorization | Low | [Mindtools Starbursting](https://www.mindtools.com/ab1w9zu/starbursting/), [MasterClass](https://www.masterclass.com/articles/starbursting) |
| **Analogical Thinking** | Cross-domain insights spark innovation | "What if X was like Y?" prompts; domain bridging visuals | High | [MIT Sloan Review](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/unlock-creativity-through-analogical-thinking/), [Board of Innovation](https://www.boardofinnovation.com/tools/analogy-thinking/) |

## Anti-Features

Features to deliberately NOT implement. Common mistakes in AI brainstorming.

### Judgment & Criticism Errors

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead | Source |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **Critique During Divergent Phase** | Kills creative flow; makes people self-censor | Visually separate divergent and convergent modes; no evaluation UI during idea generation | [IDEO Rule #1](https://www.ideou.com/blogs/inspiration/7-simple-rules-of-brainstorming) |
| **"That won't work" responses** | Shuts down contributors | "Yes, and..." language; build-on prompts | [IDEO Rules](https://designthinking.ideo.com/resources/brainstorming) |
| **Automatic idea filtering/ranking during generation** | Premature convergence | Only enable selection tools after explicit phase transition | [Double Diamond](https://david-theil.medium.com/the-double-diamond-a-model-for-divergent-and-convergent-thinking-4c902a546796) |

### Facilitation Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead | Source |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **Facilitator Monopoly** | AI dominating = user not thinking | Ask questions, don't provide answers; prompt for user ideas first | [Retrospective Anti-Patterns](https://codelucky.com/retrospective-anti-patterns/) |
| **Blank Board Start** | Overwhelming; causes analysis paralysis | Pre-structured templates; warm-up exercises; seed prompts | [Lucid Anti-Patterns](https://lucid.co/blog/collaboration-anti-patterns) |
| **Premature Convergence** | First good idea isn't best idea; "groan zone" matters | Require minimum idea count before selection; extend divergent phase | [Lucid Collaboration](https://lucid.co/blog/collaboration-anti-patterns) |
| **Forced Consensus** | Artificial agreement hides better ideas | Allow dissent; capture minority opinions | [Lucid Anti-Patterns](https://lucid.co/blog/collaboration-anti-patterns) |

### AI-Specific Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead | Source |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **AI Generates All Ideas** | User becomes passive; loses ownership | AI prompts and structures; user generates content | [Human-AI Brainstorming Research](https://aisel.aisnet.org/ecis2023_rp/429/) |
| **Free-Riding on AI** | Users stop thinking; quality drops | Require user contribution before AI assistance | [ECIS AI Collaboration Study](https://aisel.aisnet.org/ecis2023_rp/429/) |
| **Overconfident AI Suggestions** | AI ideas aren't automatically better | Frame AI contributions as "one perspective"; encourage building/modifying | [PMI AI Brainstorming](https://www.pmi.org/blog/better-brainstorming-with-ai) |
| **Generic/Obvious Prompts** | "Think outside the box" is useless | Specific, technique-based prompts with examples | [SCAMPER specificity](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/learn-how-to-use-the-best-ideation-methods-scamper) |

### Process Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead | Source |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **No Clear Problem Statement** | "Brainstorm about the product" = chaos | Require HMW question before starting; help refine if too broad/narrow | [NN/g HMW](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-might-we-questions/) |
| **Skipping Warm-Up** | Cold start = slow/shallow ideas | Always offer warm-up exercise; make it quick (5 min) | [Lucid Warm-Ups](https://lucid.co/blog/brainstorming-warm-up-exercises) |
| **No Follow-Up Actions** | Ideas without owners die | Capture next steps; assign ownership; set deadlines | [LinkedIn Best Practices](https://www.linkedin.com/advice/0/what-some-best-practices-facilitating-effective-2f) |
| **Evaluation Without Criteria** | "Pick your favorite" = bias wins | Establish criteria first; use structured evaluation | [HatRabbits](https://hatrabbits.com/en/evaluate-brainstorm-ideas/) |

## MVP Recommendation

For MVP, prioritize these table stakes:

1. **"How Might We" problem framing** - Everything depends on a clear question
2. **Visual idea capture** - Core value proposition; sticky-note metaphor
3. **Divergent/convergent phase separation** - Prevents premature critique
4. **Basic clustering** - Manual drag-to-group with theme labels
5. **Dot voting** - Simple democratic selection

Defer to post-MVP:
- Six Thinking Hats: High complexity, requires user training
- Step-Ladder Technique: Complex orchestration
- AI adaptive technique switching: Requires behavior analysis ML
- Multi-criteria weighted matrices: Adds cognitive load

### First Differentiator to Add Post-MVP

**SCAMPER prompts** - Low complexity, high value, immediately actionable. Users understand "What could we substitute?" without training.

## Technique Selection Guide

When the AI should suggest which technique:

| User Situation | Suggested Technique | Rationale |
|----------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Starting fresh, no constraints | Free brainstorm + Mind Map | Build volume first |
| Improving existing idea | SCAMPER | Structured modification |
| Stuck/blocked | Reverse Brainstorming | Inverting unlocks thinking |
| Need comprehensive coverage | Starbursting (5W1H) | Question-first ensures no gaps |
| Team disagrees on approach | Six Thinking Hats | Parallel thinking reduces conflict |
| Time-pressured | Crazy Eights or 6-3-5 | Forced rapid output |
| Need wild/breakthrough ideas | Analogical Thinking | Cross-domain sparks innovation |
| Selecting from many ideas | Dot Vote -> Impact-Effort Matrix | Democratic then structured |

## Success Metrics for AI Brainstorming

Based on research, measure:

| Metric | What It Indicates | How to Track |
|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Idea Count** | Divergent phase productivity | Count unique ideas generated |
| **Idea Diversity** | Avoiding tunnel vision | Clustering analysis; topic spread |
| **Build-On Rate** | Collaborative thinking | Ideas marked as "building on" another |
| **Participation Balance** | All voices heard | Contribution distribution |
| **Time to First Idea** | Warm-up effectiveness | Timestamp tracking |
| **Convergence Satisfaction** | Selection quality | Post-session rating |
| **Action Item Completion** | Real-world impact | Follow-up tracking |
